If you’re feeding **Alice in Wonderland** into a RAG system (with LangGraph), you want questions that actually *test retrieval + reasoning*, not just trivial recall.

Here’s a structured set of **good question types** you can use 👇

---

## 📖 1. Basic Retrieval (Sanity Check)

These confirm your RAG pipeline is working at all.

* Who is Alice?
* What happens when Alice follows the White Rabbit?
* What is the Cheshire Cat known for?
* Who is the Queen of Hearts?

👉 If these fail, your chunking / embeddings are broken.

---

## 🔍 2. Context-Specific Retrieval

These require pulling the *right chunk*, not just general knowledge.

* What advice does the Caterpillar give Alice?
* What happens at the Mad Tea Party?
* Why is the Queen angry during the croquet game?
* What does Alice find when she enters the small door?

👉 Good for testing chunk relevance + retrieval precision.

---

## 🧠 3. Multi-Chunk Reasoning

These test whether your system can combine info across chunks.

* How does Alice’s size change throughout the story, and what causes it?
* How do different characters react to Alice’s behavior?
* What pattern can you see in how authority figures behave in Wonderland?

👉 If this fails, your system retrieves but doesn’t synthesize well.

---

## 🎭 4. Character Understanding

Great for semantic search quality.

* How would you describe the personality of the Cheshire Cat?
* Compare the Queen of Hearts and the King of Hearts.
* Why does the Mad Hatter behave strangely?

---

## 🔗 5. “Why” and “How” Questions (Critical for RAG)

These expose hallucination vs grounded answers.

* Why does Alice struggle to understand Wonderland?
* How does logic differ in Wonderland compared to the real world?
* Why do conversations often feel nonsensical?

👉 These should force grounded reasoning from retrieved text.

---

## 🧪 6. Adversarial / Trap Questions

These are **very important** for RAG evaluation.

* What is Alice’s last name? ❌ (Not in text)
* What year does the story take place? ❌
* What city is Wonderland located in? ❌

👉 Good RAG answer = “Not found in the provided text.”

---

## 🧩 7. Quote-Based Retrieval

Tests exact chunk recall.

* What does the Cheshire Cat say about madness?
* Find a quote where Alice questions her identity.
* What line does the Queen repeat frequently?

---

## 🧱 8. Long-Context Questions

Stress test chunk stitching.

* Summarize Alice’s journey from falling down the rabbit hole to the trial.
* What are the major events in chronological order?

---

## ⚙️ 9. LangGraph-Specific Testing Ideas

Since you’re using LangGraph, you can test flows:

* Ask the same question twice → does it reuse memory?
* Ask a vague question → does it trigger clarification?
* Ask multi-step queries:

  * “Who does Alice meet first, and what do they tell her?”

---

## 🧠 Pro Tip (Most Important)

A *good RAG test question*:

* Requires retrieval (not common knowledge)
* Is grounded in text
* Has a verifiable answer
* Sometimes spans multiple chunks
